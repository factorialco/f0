# Definition of Done

This is the **technical contract** for an F0 component. Every component, regardless of who builds it, must satisfy these criteria to enter and stay at each maturity level. It is the source of truth referenced by the contribution and promotion skills, the design review, and the quality gate.

For the human entry-point and "where do I start", see [Contributions](/docs/about-f0-how-to-contribute--documentation).

## Phase 1 — Problem

**Entry:** anyone identifies a recurring UI need.

**Exit (DoD):** with Claude — which searches the repo and Storybook for you — you've established that:

- it doesn't already exist, and isn't covered by **extending** something that does (_look first_), and
- it's a **real, reusable need worth being in F0** for everyone — not a one-off that belongs in your product.

This step is **Claude-driven — there's no Foundations gate yet**; only genuinely new, reusable needs continue. See [`f0-component-contribution`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/vendor/skills/f0-component-contribution/SKILL.md).

---

## Phase 2 — Design

**Entry:** the need passed the filter — it's worth being in F0.

**Exit (DoD):**

- **Design it right.** Decide where it lives — Core, a Kit, or Domain specific (see [Where it goes](/docs/about-f0-where-it-goes--documentation)) — and design it **abstract and reusable**, not tied to one screen. A design is linked (a Figma, a Composer prototype, or another) with a draft API (props, variants, slots).
- **Validate with the team.** Share the proposal in `#f0-support` — the need, a Figma, a prototype, a draft PR. Foundations reviews it (design + development) and gives the OK before it's final. **Depth scales with impact:** a new component or a high-impact change (a token, icon, pattern, or new public API) gets a **design review** up front (see [Design Review](/docs/about-f0-design-review--documentation)); a low-impact change on an experimental component is a light thread and the owner decides.

Validation ends in **proceed** (build it), or — if it's not a straight yes — one of a few next steps (redirect · needs more info · lives in your product · declined), none a dead end. See [How to contribute](/docs/about-f0-how-to-contribute--documentation) for what each means.

---

## Phase 3 — Build

**Entry:** design review approved.

**Exit (DoD):**

- Component lives in `src/experimental/<Category>/F0Name/` (or `src/sds/<area>/` for domain). See the [Where it goes](/docs/about-f0-where-it-goes--documentation) page to confirm the right subfolder by intended destination.
- Story file with `tags: ["autodocs", "experimental"]`.
- Unit tests (Vitest) cover the public API.
- Storybook play function covers the primary user flow.
- Axe a11y test passes.
- MDX documentation reaches at least the **Acceptable** tier (see [`f0-docs`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/vendor/skills/f0-docs/SKILL.md)).
- `f0-quality-gate` skill passes (typecheck, lint, format, tests).
- PR merged.

---

## Phase 4 — Real use

**Entry:** component released as `experimental` for at least one F0 version.

**Exit (DoD) — two axes + re-confirmation:**

**Demand axis (must hit all):**

- **Core & Kit:** used by **≥3 product teams** in production (verified via cross-repo search in the `factorialco` org). **Domain specific:** adopted across its domain's surfaces — the ≥3-teams bar doesn't apply, since it's scoped to one domain by design.
- No "would you remove this?" pushback from consumers in the last 30 days.

**Maturity axis (must hit all):**

- **No breaking changes for ≥60 days.**
- **Zero open critical or high-severity bugs.**
- `f0-quality-gate` passes including extended subagent reviews (a11y, design tokens, dark mode).
- Component owner declares the API stable in the #f0-support promotion thread.

**Foundations re-confirmation:**

- **Core & Kit:** Foundations explicitly approves promotion (no auto-promotion). **Domain specific:** the owning team promotes it, in place.

<Callout>
There is no time minimum beyond the 60-day no-breaking-changes window. A widely-adopted component can promote in 2 months; a niche one may stay experimental for a year.
</Callout>

---

## Phase 5 — Promote

**Entry:** Phase 4 DoD met.

**Exit (DoD):**

- **Core & Kit:** component moved to `src/components/` (or `patterns/`, `kits/`, `layouts/`, `hooks/`) following the [`f0-experimental-component-migration`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/vendor/skills/f0-experimental-component-migration/SKILL.md) skill. **Domain specific promotes in place** — it stays in `src/sds/<area>/`, no move.
- Story `tags` updated to `["autodocs", "stable"]`.
- MDX docs at **Good** tier minimum (Gold encouraged).
- **Core & Kit:** ownership transferred to Foundations in `CODEOWNERS`. A **Domain specific** component stays owned by its team.
- Promotion announced in `#f0-support` Slack and changelog.

**Response time:** an F0 designer + Foundations engineer respond to promotion requests based on team availability and as quickly as possible — we don't commit to a fixed SLA today, as it depends on team size and current focus. For urgent or blocking cases, ping `#f0-support` in Slack.

---

## Maturity levels

Three levels — **experimental → stable → deprecated** — with the **story tag** as the source of truth. See [Components Maturity](/docs/about-f0-components-maturity--documentation) for what each one guarantees, who owns it, and where it lives.

---

## Deprecation

Deprecation requires three JSDoc tags (`@deprecated` / `@removeIn` / `@migration`) and a migration guide; removal is no sooner than a quarter (≥90 days). Full policy and the exact tags: [Release & Versioning](/docs/about-f0-release-and-versioning--documentation).
