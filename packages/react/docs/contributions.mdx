# Contributing to F0

Contributing to F0 happens **in the chat with your AI agent** — it reads the repo and walks you through it. F0 is open source — anyone can use it and shape it — but it grows **intentionally**: we add things with clear cross-team value, and say no with a documented reason and an alternative. Below are the steps, and each one has prompts you can drop straight into your agent. (F0 ships **AGENTS.md** and skills, so any AGENTS.md-aware agent works — Claude Code, Codex, OpenCode, or whatever you use.)

<KeyPoint eyebrow="Start here" title="Tell your agent what you want to build">
Open **your agent** in the `f0` repo and say what you need — e.g. _"I need X in F0 — is there anything already, and if not, what process should I follow to contribute it?"_ It takes it from there and walks you through the steps below.
</KeyPoint>

## The steps, from idea to F0

### 1 · Look first

_Ask your agent, or browse Storybook._

- Understand what F0 already has.
- Check if you can use — or extend — something that already exists.

> **Ask your agent** — _"Is there already an F0 component, kit or pattern for X?"_ · _"Could I cover X by extending an existing component instead?"_

### 2 · Is it worth being in F0 for everyone?

- Is it new, or an evolution of something that exists?
- Is it a real need — not a one-off?
- Will other teams or product areas use it too?

> If it's a one-off for a single screen with no reuse, build it in your product from F0 pieces — a valid outcome, not a rejection.

### 3 · Design it right

_Your agent suggests · Foundations confirms._

- Decide where it lives — Core, a Kit, or Domain specific (see [Where it goes](/docs/about-f0-where-it-goes--documentation)).
- Build it abstract, so other teams can reuse it — not tied to your screen.

> **Ask your agent** — _"Where should X live — Core, a Kit, or Domain specific?"_ · _"How do I build X so other teams can reuse it?"_

### 4 · Validate with the team

- Share the proposal in [#f0-support](https://factorialteam.slack.com/archives/C082ZNKS403) — bring whatever fits: the need, a Figma, a prototype, a draft PR.
- Foundations reviews it (design + development) and gives the OK before it's final.

> **Ask your agent** — _"Draft a #f0-support message proposing X: what it is, where it'd live, and what I'm bringing."_

**Depth scales with impact** — a prop or an icon is light; a shared pattern needs buy-in; changing something already in use needs a migration plan (see [Release & Versioning](/docs/about-f0-release-and-versioning--documentation)). Match the process to the blast radius, not the amount of code.

Once it's validated, you build it to the [Definition of Done](/docs/about-f0-definition-of-done--documentation) — it ships as **experimental**, and once it's adopted, it's promoted to **stable** (Core & Kit by Foundations; a Domain specific component in place, by its own team).

## Reporting a bug

Fixing a bug, or something looks broken? That's **not a new contribution**. Ask your agent for the owner (or check [CODEOWNERS](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/CODEOWNERS)), open a PR with steps to reproduce, and flag it in [#f0-support](https://factorialteam.slack.com/archives/C082ZNKS403) if it's blocking you.

## Maturity levels

F0 has **three** maturity levels: **Experimental**, **Stable**, and **Deprecated**. See the [Component Maturity model](/docs/about-f0-components-maturity--documentation) for what each one guarantees and what it requires.

## Who does what

F0 is built and maintained by the **Foundations team**, with contributions from every product team. Three roles matter in the flow:

- **Foundations** — owns Core (`components/`, `patterns/`, `ui/`, `kits/`, `layouts/`, `hooks/`) and the shared **tokens** and **icons**. They review what enters F0, and **approve and promote Core & Kit to stable**. (A **Domain specific** component is its team's to promote, in place.) Reach them in [#f0-support](https://factorialteam.slack.com/archives/C082ZNKS403).
- **Your team** — builds the contribution and **owns and maintains it**. While it's experimental, your team is its **CODEOWNER** (add its path to [`CODEOWNERS`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/CODEOWNERS)) and fields its bugs. On promotion, a Core/Kit component's ownership transfers to Foundations; a **Domain specific** component stays yours.
- **Your agent** — the **access layer**: it searches, filters, guides placement, and builds to the standard. It does **not** approve, mark anything stable, or merge on your behalf — those are human / Foundations calls.

**Source of truth:** the repo — code, [`CODEOWNERS`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/CODEOWNERS), and these docs — is authoritative. If your agent ever seems to contradict them, the repo wins; treat its output as a suggestion to verify, not a ruling.

## Working with AI agents

Your agent is the **entry point** to F0. F0 ships **AGENTS.md** and skills in the repo, so any AGENTS.md-aware agent (Claude Code, Codex, OpenCode, …) reads the repo, searches Storybook, filters your request, and drives the build. Each step has a matching **skill** that tells it exactly how F0 does things:

| Task                              | Skill                                                                                                                       |
| --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Propose & build a new component   | [`f0-component-contribution`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/vendor/skills/f0-component-contribution/SKILL.md)  |
| Promote experimental → stable     | [`f0-component-promotion`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/vendor/skills/f0-component-promotion/SKILL.md)        |
| Write Storybook stories           | [`f0-storybook-stories`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/vendor/skills/f0-storybook-stories/SKILL.md)            |
| Write MDX documentation           | [`f0-docs`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/vendor/skills/f0-docs/SKILL.md)                                      |
| Pre-commit quality checks         | [`f0-quality-gate`](https://github.com/factorialco/f0/blob/main/vendor/skills/f0-quality-gate/SKILL.md)                      |
